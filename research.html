    Projects of HCI

    VAHF

    CHI23: Enabling Voice-Accompanying Hand-to-Face Gesture Recognition with Cross-Device Sensing

    VAHF investigated voice-accompanying hand-to-face (VAHF) gestures for voice interaction and facilitated VAHF gesture recognition through a novel cross-device sensing method that leverages multiple channels (vocal, ultrasound, and IMU) of data from commodity devices (earbuds, watches, and rings).

    ConeSpeech

    TVCG2023: ConeSpeech: Exploring Directional Speech Interaction for Multi-Person Remote Communication in Virtual Reality

    ConeSpeech is a virtual reality (VR) based multi-user remote communication technique, which enables users to selectively speak to target listeners without distracting bystanders. With ConeSpeech, the user looks at the target listener and only in a cone-shaped area in the direction can the listeners hear the speech.
